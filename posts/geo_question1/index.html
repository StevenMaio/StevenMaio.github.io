A Geometry Question

2020-07-17Steven Maio
#geometry  #problems 

In my work with a professor I've come across this problem and I can't solve it. Problem is worded after the edit.

Problem: Suppose that $\gamma : I \to \mathbb{R}^2$ ($I$ is some interval) is a simple convex closed planar cuve, whose curvature is bounded above and below by positive constants, that is $$ 0 < A \le k(s) \le B < \infty $$ for $s \in I$, and let $D$ be the open region bounded by $\gamma$. Then there exists $c > 0$ such that for any $x \in D$ and $r < \text{diam } \gamma$ we have that $$ m_2 ( B(x;r) \cap D) \ge c r^2, $$ where $m_2$ is 2-d Lebesgue measure and $B(x;r) = \{ y \in \mathbb{R}^2 \ : \ | x - y | < r \}$.

I posted the question here. One of the important points is that because the curvature is bounded above, this limits how fast $\gamma$ can turn, which means that for those points inside $D$ really close to $\gamma$, there will be 'room' in the opposite direction. I think this suggests that for some any point $x \in \gamma$, we can find some disk whose radius relates to $B$ contained inside $D$ and which intersects $\gamma$ only at $x$.

Feel free to respond there, or hit me up with a message on GitHub if you can help.

Edit (7/21/20): Well, I got the statement of the problem wrong, but I think original statement implies the hypothesis of my original statement. The statement is:

Problem: Let $\gamma : I \to \mathbb{R}^2$ be a closed simple curve such that there
exists $C > 0 $ such that for any two points $a,b \in \text{Im }\gamma$ we have that $\text{len}(a,b) \le C | b - a|$, where $\text{len}(a,b)$ is the length of the shortest arc between $a$ and $b$ on $\gamma$. Then there exists $c > 0$ such that for all $p \in D$, where $D$ is the open region bounded by $\gamma$, and $r < \text{diam } \gamma$, then $$ \text{Area}\Big(B(p;r) \cap D\Big) \ge c r^2. $$
